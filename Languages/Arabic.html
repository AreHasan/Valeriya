Arabic

This is a language that I developed an interest for when I began studying in university.

The reason I developed interest for it, is because I knew it was vastly different from any other language I had previous knowledge in. Additionally, it is one of the most widely spoken languages out there, and I figured it would be a useful language to add to my language plate (language plate referring to the languages I would be able to speak fluently).

This is why, upon finding the opportunity to study Arabic at a language workshop at my university, I begin to do so. I started by attending the language group meetings, where we began with the alphabet, and then moved onto greetings, useful words and terminology.

I was very fixated on the alphabet, and recall barely taking any notes after that, because I was so focussed on trying to memorize each letter, and how it changed dependant of its position in a word in Arabic script. To be frank, I am still stuck on this part, because of lack of time I can allocate towards it, and the fact that I’m currently prioritizing Turkish as my primary language goal.

I hope to soon be able to master the alphabet, and script, so I can move onto the grammar and vocabulary of the language.

I also attempted to learn a little bit about the phonetics behind it, but was not able to find many resources.

Another obstacle I came across was the lack of ability to decide which dialect I wanted to focus on. Currently, I’m debating between the Lebanese and the Egyptian dialect. Still in a pickle with this one.

I would hope to progress a little more going into the next school year, and make Arabic my next language goal after Turkish.
