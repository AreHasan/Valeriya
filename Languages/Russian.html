Russian

Russian is my other native language, and it was the first language I was ever exposed to growing up. I am able to comprehend and express myself in it well, however, I do have minor setbacks, particularly when it comes to more narrow terminology, as this is not something I studied growing up in Canadian schools.

Around the age of eight I was concerned with my ability to read in my native language, so I remember picking out a children’s book, and reading it over and over until I was able to read it with fluency. Fluency referring to the fluidity of my speech (no stutters or odd pauses). I then switched to another book, and repeated the same process until I got through all of my children’s books.

Then, I recall asking my mother for help to learn Russian grammar, and how to write in Russian cursive. She did so for a bit, however at a certain point she was unable to continue to help me. I did not let this stop me, and I took it upon myself to continue alone with workbooks from back home until roughly the age of 10.

The following couple of years, I began researching ways I could improve my Russian. I came across a Russian Language School in Vancouver. The opportunity to attend the school was something I requested for my parents as my Christmas gift. My wish was granted, and I had begun attending the school shortly before turning 14. At the school, I rapidly acquired knowledge on Russian grammar, and progressed in my comprehension, and ability to express myself in the language.

Post graduating, I feel more deeply connected with my culture and native language. I am able to read, write, understand, and freely express myself in the language with little difficulty. I did not always feel this way growing up, and I am proud to have put so much time and work into gaining this reconnection with my culture so early in my life.

My culture and language are the roots of me. It is important to me to be in tune and connected with that part of my identity, and this is why I regularly make an effort to stay connected with the Russian-speaking community around me.
