English

English is one of my two native languages.

Around the age of three, I moved to Canada with my family. I began attending a Montessori school, where my education was provided in both English and Russian. Thanks to this, I was able to acquire both languages simultaneously. I know I rapidly progressed in my acquisition of English, because I remember English feeling like my first language.

As I grew older, I began regularly visiting the library with my sister and father. My father encouraged us to read, and this is where a new hobby of mine developed. I went big and hard on reading, and as a result, acquired greater and richer vocabulary with each day.

Despite my full integration into an English-speaking environment, English is my least favourite language, as I find it rather unpleasant to the ear, and mostly senseless grammatically. I tend to avoid to use it when possible.

English is definitely my strongest language in terms of both comprehension and expression. It is the language I encounter the most throughout my day-to-day life. I am the most at ease with it, and can effectively communicate my needs and feelings with it.
